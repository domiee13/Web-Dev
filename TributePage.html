Freddie Mercury

Here's a timeline of Freddie Mercury's life:

  • 1946 — Farrokh Bulsara (Freddie Mercury) was born in Zanzibar, Tanzania on September 5, 1946
  • 1954 — At the Age of eight, Farrokh began his education at St. Peters English boarding school (near Bombay, India)
  • 1958 — While in school, Farrokh began referring to himself as Freddie, and in 1958, he formed a music group, The Hectics.
  • 1964 — Freddie Mercury moved to Middlesex, England in 1964, at the age of 17.
  • 1967 — Freddie joined the band Smile with Roger Talyor and Brian May, and Tim Staffel.
  • 1970 — In 1970, Freddie Mercury took over as the lead singer of Smile, and renamed the band Queen.
  • 1975 — The album A Night At The Opera was released
  • 1975 — Freddie Mercury toured Japan with Queen
  • 1984 — Queen decided to take a break. During that break, Freddie Mercury decided to release a solo album called Love Kills.
  • 1988 — The Open Air festival in Barcelona, on October 8th, was Freddie Mercury's last performance.
  • 1991 — The album Innuendo was released in 1991
  • 1991 — November 23, 1991, the day before he died, Freddie Mercury announced that he had AIDS.
